What is BOBA?

> Bubble milk tea, also known as pearl tea or boba tea, has a tea base mixed with either milk or fruit.

> While many people believe the name bubble comes from the tapioca pearls, it actually comes from the bubbles produced when the beverage is shaken before it is served.

> While it was not an original component, most of this tea will come with small chewy tapioca balls or boba.

History of BOBA

> Bubble milk tea was originally created in the 1980s in tea shops across Taiwan.

> One tea shop owner added the tapioca pearls which has now become synonymous with bubble milk tea.

> There are now hundreds of different recipes in tea shops across the globe.


How did it become famous?

> It is difficult to identify exactly why this beverage has become a global phenomenon but many people point to its unique texture and appearance.

> The tapioca pearls stand out as they are typically black.

> Although they can be white or transparent as well. The color of the tapioca is determined by its ingredients.
